# Brand Identity & Copy Guidelines: FYP Management Platform

## Voice & Tone: The Institutional Guide
The voice of the FYP Management Platform is **authoritative, clear, and dependable**. It avoids the playful snark of modern SaaS or the robotic coldness of legacy academic software. Instead, it speaks with the quiet confidence of a trusted mentor.

### Core Principles
1. **Academic Authority**: Use precise, professional terminology. No slang or "app-speak."
2. **Ruthless Clarity**: Information density must be high, but legibility remains paramount.
3. **Calm Guidance**: In moments of stress (deadlines, errors), the tone remains steady and helpful.

## Tone Selection by Context

| Context | Tone | Style |
|---------|------|-------|
| Dashboard Overview | Neutral/Descriptive | High-level summaries. |
| Submission Forms | Instructional | Direct verbs, clear field labels. |
| Error Messages | Empathetic/Direct | State the problem, then the fix. |
| Success Toasts | Brief/Understated | "Submission received" instead of "Awesome job!" |

## Plain Speech Rules (Default)
- **Use Title Case for headings**: "Project Proposal Management"
- **Use Sentence case for labels**: "Upload final document"
- **Active Voice**: "Submit your proposal" rather than "Your proposal can be submitted here."
- **Omit "Please"**: It adds unnecessary clutter to an institutional tool.

## Sentry-Inspired Personality Moments (Subtle)
While primarily functional, we add personality in low-stakes areas:
- **Empty States**: "No active milestones. A quiet moment before the next phase."
- **404 Pages**: "This coordinate does not exist in the project registry. Let's return to the dashboard."
- **Login**: "Welcome back to the register. Authenticating identity..."

## Punctuation & Formatting
- **No exclamation marks** in primary UI.
- **Hyphens** for compound words (e.g., "real-time").
- **En-dashes** for date/mark ranges (e.g., "75–85%").
- **Currency**: Use PKR or USD as required by the university context.

## Prohibited Terms
- Avoid "Oops!" or "Whoops!"
- Avoid "Supercharge" or "Game-changing."
- Avoid "Click here" (use descriptive action labels like "Download Rubric").
